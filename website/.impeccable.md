# AI Sensei 3000 — Landing Page

## Design Context

### Users
General macOS users and anime fans browsing on a laptop or desktop, usually during downtime or right after seeing the app mentioned on X / YouTube / a friend. They are not developers and not trying to evaluate a tool — they're curious about a cute anime character that lives on their screen and helps them with stuff. The job on this page: *make them smile, show them what it does in a few seconds, get them to click Download*. Also tease paid character themes as a secondary action.

### Brand Personality
Delightful, magical, playful. Warm and character-forward — the product *is* the character, not a feature list. Voice is casual, bilingual EN/JP accent (Japanese as flourish, never a translation burden), first-person friendly ("she opens a portal," not "the app overlays a cursor marker"). Emoji-light; rely on illustration and typography for charm.

### Aesthetic Direction
Kawaii / pastel storybook. Soft dual palette — pink and blue as equal co-stars, supported by mint + soft gold — over a cream-washed sky. Rounded everything (pill buttons, 28-40px card radii), chunky rounded display type (`Baloo 2`), balanced with a softer bilingual body face (`M PLUS Rounded 1c`). Sticker-cut character illustrations float on soft gradient surfaces. Light theme only — this is a sunny, morning-study feel, never a dark tool.

**Anti-references:** generic AI tool landing pages, Linear/Notion-clone minimalism, dark SaaS with neon accents, crypto-cute, anything that reads "for the dev Twitter crowd." No technical jargon, no API screenshots, no terminal aesthetics.

### Design Principles
1. **Character first, features second.** Every section should have a character or asset presence. A headline without a character nearby is underweight.
2. **Bilingual as flourish, not furniture.** Japanese subtitles beneath English headings add atmosphere; they should never carry primary information, never wrap awkwardly, always render as their own line.
3. **Pink AND blue.** Neither dominates. Alternate card tones, never let a whole section lean one hue — the duality is the brand.
4. **Rounded and soft, but not mushy.** Generous border radii and drop shadows are OK because they're doing the kawaii work — but spacing rhythm and typographic hierarchy stay tight and intentional. Polish beats cute.
5. **No dev-tool tells.** No monospace, no terminal mock-ups, no feature-matrix tables, no "trusted by" logo walls. If it could appear on a YC company page, rework it.
